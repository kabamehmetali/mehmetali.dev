The Evolution of Humanity and the Future of Software

By Mehmet Ali Kaba - March 15, 2025

Since the moment humanity produced its first intellectual creation, it has gone through numerous historical stages. We have established different societies, reached various levels of civilization, and throughout these processes, our physical and intellectual evolution has continued. From the Stone Age to the Agricultural Revolution, and then to the Industrial Revolution, these historical transformations have fundamentally changed how societies work and live. For instance, in agrarian societies, the need for more labor led families to have many children. However, with the Machine Age in the 20th century, agricultural activities became largely mechanized, reducing the need for human labor.

Thanks to this mechanization, the workforce that was once heavily employed in agriculture began to shift toward other fields, using machines that ranged from simple elevators to complex technologies. This shift enabled the construction of massive structures and achievements in areas like engineering and architecture. Today, in the 21st century, automating machines and equipping them with artificial intelligence have become more critical than ever. The millions of years of evolution in living organisms seem to parallel the gradual development of machinery and software technology.

Just as past revolutions dramatically altered the course of human history, I believe the era of artificial intelligence will also lead to significant changes in our future. Every innovation that reduces physical labor ultimately highlights our intellectual capabilities. Therefore, I consider every investment in software to be immensely valuable for future social and technological progress. Software and artificial intelligence will not only transform the business or industrial sectors but also revolutionize every aspect of daily life, opening the doors to an entirely new era.
